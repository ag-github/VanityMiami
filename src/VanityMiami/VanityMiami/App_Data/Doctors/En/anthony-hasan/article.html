History of the TUBA (Trans-umbilical Breast Augmentation)

The first TUBA (Trans-umbilical Breast Augmentation) was described in 1992. Before that time there were other ways that breast implants were performed. The following is a history of the procedures, and how they changed over the years.

· T. D. Cronin and F.F. Gerow, two American plastic surgeons used silicon gel implants in 1964. These implants were inserted in the woman’s breast where they meet the chest cavity. Many women completed the procedure with great results. They felt better about the way they looked in their chest area.
· J. Jenny described breasts implants that completed under the nipple – areola complex in 1972. This would give women a smaller scar than the previous procedure, which many women to find a plus.
· Koeller described using an incision in the axilla for a tansaxillary breast implant in 1973.
· In 1976, a breast implant that used the abdominal tunnel technique was described by using empty saline implants. Many women that felt uncomfortable having a scar in the breast area welcomed having the scar in the abdominal area.
· The TUBA (Trans-umbilical Breast Augmentation) using the navel insertion approach was in 1992. Dr. Hasan was a pioneer in this type of procedure for breast augmentation. It was definitely the most popular of all the types of breast implantations because of the very small scar it would leave by the naval.

The TUBA (Trans-umbilical Breast Augmentation) Surgical Technique

The TUBA (Trans-umbilical Breast Augmentation) surgical technique differs from other breast implant techniques because it uses an incision that is placed at the navel of the woman. Since this procedure makes it possible for the doctor to produce the breast implant in the breast pocket by tunneling up to the breast pocket, there is no scarring near the breasts. The insertion is small at the navel. It is only two centimeters long. For this reason, the pre – filled silicone – gel implants cannot be inserted because they are not able to go through an incision that is that small. This also makes it more difficult than traditional implant to be performed.

Advantages of the TUBA (Trans-umbilical Breast Augmentation)

Many women love the way the TUBA (Trans-umbilical Breast Augmentation) makes their breasts look. They also find that scar is down around their navel which is harder to be seen by anyone else. This gives them the confidence they desire when they want to have their breasts enlarged without the scarring in the breast area.
