Liposuction Miami and Introduction to Liposuction Surgery in Miami


Introduction to Liposuction Miami Surgery

Liposuction, or lipoplasty, is a cosmetic surgical procedure in which excess fatty tissue is removed from under the skin by suction. This procedure has become very popular and is considered to be the number one procedure requested to plastic surgeons today. The procedure can place greater accentuation on the body’s natural contours. Liposuction uses a hollow stainless steel tube known as a cannula and a suction device known as an aspirator to remove fat from the body’s subcutaneous deep fat layer through a variety of different possible techniques. Liposuction surgery can be used to treat fat pockets in many parts of the body including: abdomen and waist, thighs, hips and buttocks, arms, neck and back, chest, cheeks and chin, and calves and ankles.
Liposuction is not a treatment for obesity and is not a replacement for regular exercise and good eating habits.



In some cases, liposuction is performed alone, in other cases it is used with other plastic surgery procedures such as a facelift, breast reduction, or a tummy tuck.

Benefits of Liposuction

Patients benefit both mentally and physically from liposuction. It is considered to be a safe and effective way to remove excess fat from the body. The benefits of liposuction includes, but is not limited to the improvement in the appearance of; cellulite, safe removal of fat, improvement of health usually associated with fat loss, sculpting areas of the body that even exercise do not seem to impact, and boost of self-esteem.

Ideal Candidates for Liposuction in Miami

Healthy individuals who have decided the liposuction will help them achieve their goals and do not have any medical condition or life-threatening sickness that can impair healing would be ideal for this procedure. Non- smoking patients bothered by excess fat deposits and individuals with a positive outlook and specific idea in mind for body contouring are also good candidates for liposuction.

How does Liposuction work?

Liposuction can be used for small areas, such as the space under the chin, or larger areas, such as buttocks and thighs. In both cases, a narrow tube (cannula) is inserted through a tiny incision, and used to vacuum the fat that lies deep beneath the skin. The cannula is pushed then pulled through the fat layer, breaking up the fat cells and suctioning them out by a vacuum pump or a large syringe. As the patients lose fluids along with fat, they are given intravenous fluids and carefully monitored.

Types of Liposuction Procedures

There are different and improved techniques to make Liposuction safer, easier, and less painful. These techniques consider the following:

Traditional Liposuction:

Suction Assisted Liposuction (SAL)

What is it?

It is a traditional method that uses a small cannula attached to a pressurized vacuum. The surgeon operates the cannula manually. The medicated saline solution is introduced into the area being treated and small incisions are made into the layer of fat. The cannula is inserted through the incisions and manipulated, which breaks up fat so it can be suctioned out of the body.

Advantages

Suction Assisted Liposuction is the best way to remove huge fat masses in short time. That is the reason it is used in correction of large body areas such as abdomen, buttocks or back. One more quality which attracts patients to choose traditional liposuction is the price. Compared with other types of liposuction, expenses for SAL can be ten times lower. Some medical conditions may also benefit from liposuction, including gynecomastia, where fatty breast tissue has developed in men, as well as lipomas, benign fatty tumors. Results may last permanently depending on the patient’s eating habits. It is important to maintain good physical health as well.

Risks of Suction Assisted Liposuction

Like any other medical procedure, there may be complications involved. Before you have liposuction, you should be aware of the risks and benefits based on your own personal value system.
Risks can go from swelling or numbness in the area, irritation where the cannula is inserted, and color changes in the treated area. Fluid imbalance is another complication that may occur which staff will be watching for while the patient is still in the office. However, this may happen after you go home. Swelling may also occur after liposuction. In some cases, swelling may persist for weeks or months after liposuction. Embolism is also possible when fat is loosened and enters the blood and pieces of fat get trapped in the blood vessels, gather in the lungs, or travel to the brain.

Power Assisted Liposuction (PAL)

What is it?

This method consists of a mechanical cannula that extracts fat with smaller incisions and a more precise and shorter surgical experience. It reduces the amount of manual force and physical effort required to perform lipoplasty, especially in areas with fibrous tissue. This innovation does not use heat. Therefore, there are no concerns for the accidental burning that can occur with other systems. The result is a more comfortable experience for both the surgeon and the patient.

Advantages

During Power Assisted Liposuction, doctors do not need to use much force since the vibrating cannula helps release fat cells from the body. Bruising and recovery time are significantly short. The Power Assisted Liposuction procedure takes less time to perform than manual liposuction; thus, complications and risks are reduced.

Risks of a Power Assisted Liposuction

There are minimal risks associated with the Power Assisted Liposuction. Patients can experience loose, uneven and/or wrinkled skin, which may tighten over time. Some patients may experience moderate changes in skin pigmentation.

Ultrasonic Assisted Liposuction (UAL)

What is it?

Unlike lasers and suction, this technique uses ultrasound to liquefy the fat, making it easier to remove. This method is believed to be more efficient and less traumatic than other procedures. This operation can be done in two different fashions. The fat can be drained as it gets liquefied, or it can be drained after it gets liquidized. This method can be done in the back, abdomen, and male breast region.

Advantages

Ultrasound Assisted Liposuction produces smoother, softer results. It gives the doctor more control over shaping the contours of the body. It also reduces traumas, bruising, and bleeding damages. Both men and women can benefit from this plastic surgery. UAL allows better definition of areas like waist, rib cage, and arms.

Risks of an Ultrasonic Assisted Liposuction Miami

While effectively treating the fat, continuous ultrasound energy can burn the patient’s skin. This is not a frequent event.

Laser Liposuction

What is it?

Laser liposuction is minimally invasive treatment that can help patients get rid of localized fat without undergoing a traditional liposuction treatment. This type of liposuction can treat areas such as knees, back, chest, abdomen, hips, thighs, and male breasts. Compared to traditional liposuction methods, laser liposuction results in less bleeding, bruising, and recovery time.

Advantages

Laser liposuction works great in all the cases irrespective of the body types. The laser beam helps in fine tuning the body to give the patients best outlooks. Since laser liposuction is non-invasive it has a shorter recovery period. Patients can usually get back to work just after a day’s rest. The effects of laser liposuction are permanent since the fat cells are inactivated by the laser beam, leaving them incapable of storing more fat. Another benefit of laser liposuction is that it aids in growth of new collagen which gives the skin a more turgid and vital look. The laser also assists in sealing the smaller damaged blood vessels, reducing bleeding and inflammation.

Risks of Laser Liposuction

Laser Liposuction involves thermal energy, thus, the risk of blistering and burning tissues does exist. This could result in infection and scarring. The risk of irregularities in the skin and contour are also present; patients may experience wavy skin texture or dimpling mainly in the areas that have been treated. In addition, there could be dark or light skin discoloration that fades with the passing time or in worst cases require other treatments.

Recovery

Like any other procedure, liposuction has a recovery period. The liposuction recovery period can last anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks. The recovery can be uncomfortable, and during the first few weeks most people experience varying degrees of pain, burning, swelling, and temporary numbness. During this period, it is important to know how to care for the body parts that were treated. These areas get wrapped firmly. The doctor may recommend wraps or compression garments several weeks after surgery to expedite the recovery. This also helps better tone the body and reduce swelling. Bandages will be changed after one to two days and any drains and sutures left in after liposuction will be removed after five to ten days. The stitches will either dissolve or be removed by the surgeon eight or ten days after the surgery. After a few weeks, the patient should feel comfortable enough to remove the compression garment. Swelling will continue to go down and the final result will start to be revealed. Doctors may recommend antibiotics to reduce the risk of infection at the incision sites.
