Blepharoplasty Frequently Asked Questions

Where are the incisions eyelid surgery localized?
For the upper eyelid incisions are usually made in the natural lines and creases of the eyelids, while lower eyelid incisions are made along the line of the eye and its folds.
Are there scars after blepharoplasty surgery?
If the operation is with laser procedures there will not be incisions, but if is a traditional surgery with the scalpel, incisions will be made, although these will be in the most confidential position and disappear with the passing time.
How long until I can return to work after a blepharoplasty surgery?
Usually patients can return to work a week after the procedure.
How long until I can take off the stitches?
The stitches are usually removed three to five days after the procedure of eyelids cosmetic surgery of the, although they may disappear on their own.
Should I stop taking my medications for blood pressure, thyroid, before my surgery?
It is very important to communicate with your surgeon about the medications you are taking. You should have your blood pressure checked, your heart, your sugar, your thyroid, this is imperative for a successful and without complication surgery.
Can I go through an eyelid surgery if I have an eye disease?
It is very important to be sure if you have had surgery on the eyes before, if you cannot wear contact lenses, if your eyes lagrimea much, if you have dry eye and other important information. Sometimes it would be necessary to control these diseases first by an ophthalmologist before surgery.
There may be infection or ulcer after double eyelid surgery?
As in any surgery if you do not have the best conditions and did not have the most experience doctors there could be infection, which could sometimes be control with antibiotics before and after surgery. Corneal ulcer is rare, it happens due to edema of the eyelids. Drops or artificial tears stand for a few days will be the measures taken to reduce edema of the eyelids.
